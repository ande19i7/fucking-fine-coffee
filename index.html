Our approach to gourmet coffee

Why we are unique, bitches

First, it is fucking important for us that we are Fair Trade certified. That means that we are supporting a better life for farming families in the developing world through fair prices, direct trade, community development and environmental stewardship.

Second, we make amazing coffee at a fair price for you guys. This is how we do it.

We are using the slow-roasting method which achieves a better cup of coffee in the end. This, in a combination with high-quality beans from Columbia and Brazil, not to forget our unique machine, is what that makes our coffee heavenly good.

The world-class barista, Luigi Bonta, has designed our coffee roasting machine which is absolutely next level. It is designed to track down every single coffee seed that is not ideal for your cup of coffee, and then it will get rid of it. This will result in the best coffee you can ever imagine. On top of that it is being roasted here in Copenhagen, Vesterbro. Your new local roaster?

Furthermore, we are not freakin’ overpricing our products like all other coffee roasters are. We are focusing on giving you a fair price for a nice product. Somehow, we have combined quantity and quality. We do love you students!

Recipes for nerds

Better brew it correct

A key moment in the journey from crop to cup is the process of brewing. All the hard work up until this point, all the potential and deliciousness locked within the coffee can be lost by bad brewing. It is upsetting how easy it is to brew coffee badly, but understanding the basic principles can lead to better results and make the process more enjoyable.

Espresso

Weight of ground coffee per cup:

19 grams

Amount of liquid you should produce from that coffee:

42 grams

How long the brewing process should take:

25-29 seconds

How hot the water should be for brewing:

90°c
